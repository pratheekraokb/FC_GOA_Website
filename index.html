About FC GOA

FC Goa, established in 2014, stands as a prominent football club in the Indian Super League (ISL), competing at the highest level of professional football in India. The team, based in Goa, calls the Fatorda Stadium in Margao its home ground. Owned by a partnership involving Goan Football Club Pvt. Ltd., Viacom18 Media Pvt. Ltd., and Mr. Jaydev Mody, FC Goa has consistently showcased its competitive edge in the ISL. The club has made notable appearances in AFC competitions, including the prestigious AFC Champions League. The vibrant orange and blue colors represent FC Goa, and their anthem, "Forca Goa," resonates with the passionate fanbase. The team's success has been attributed to a combination of talented players, with notable names such as Ferran Corominas, Hugo Boumous, and Edu Bedia, contributing to its legacy. While coaching staff and player rosters may have evolved since my last update, FC Goa continues to be a formidable force in Indian football.

The Fatorda Stadium, also known as The Pandit Jawaharlal Nehru Stadium in Margao, serves as the premier home venue for Goa. Since its inauguration in 1989, this iconic stadium has played host to numerous international games, including critical qualifiers for both the FIFA World Cup and AFC Asian Cup for the India national football team. Notably, The Fatorda Stadium was selected as one of the six host venues for the prestigious 2017 FIFA U-17 World Cup held in India, showcasing its significance on the global football stage.

Recognizing the need for modernization, the stadium underwent extensive refurbishment and upgrades in 2014, aligning it with the latest FIFA specifications. This redevelopment was specifically geared towards preparing the venue for the Lusofonia Games held in the same year. Boasting a seating capacity of 20,000 spectators, The Fatorda Stadium features a design that encompasses a roof covering 100 percent of the seating area. The stadium complex is thoughtfully laid out, providing two levels of fan seating arrangements, complemented by a dedicated VIP area. The strategic enhancements not only cater to the demands of international sporting events but also contribute to the overall fan experience, solidifying The Fatorda's standing as a key hub for football in the region.
